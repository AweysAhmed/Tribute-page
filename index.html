Dr.Patricia Bath

Tribute Page


  • 1942 - She was born in Harlem, New York.
  • 1958 - One of a few students to attend a cancer research workshop that was sponsored by the National Science Foundation
  • 1960 - Won the "Merit Award" of Mademoiselle Magazine for her contribution to program at the National Science Foundation
  • 1964 - Earned her Bachelors from Hunter College
  • 1968 - Graduated with Honors from Howard University Medical School
  • 1968 - Started her internship at Harlem Hospital
  • 1969 - Started her fellowship in ophthamology at Columbia University
  • 1973 - Became the first African-American to complete residency in ophthamolgoy
  • 1974 - Moved to California to work as an assitant professor of surgery at Charles R. Drew University and the Univeristy of Caifornia, Los Angeles
  • 1975 - Became the first female faculty member in the department of Ophthamology at UCLA's Jules Stein Eye institute
  • 1976- Co-founded the American institute for the prevention of Blindness. It established that "eyesight is a basic human right"
  • 1981 - Began working on her most well-known invention: the Laserphaco probe
  • 1983 - Helped create the Ophthamology Residency Training program at UCLA-Drew
  • 1986 - Completed her work on the invention of the Laserphaco probe
  • 1988 - Received a patent for her invention of the Laserphaco probe. This also made her the first African-American female doctor to receive a patent for medical purposes
  • 1993 - Retired from the UCLA Medical Center and then became an honorary member of their staff
  • 1983 - Was named a "Howard University Pioneer in Acadmeic Medicine
